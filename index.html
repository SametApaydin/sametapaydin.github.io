Samet Apaydın

Postdoctoral Researcher · Sabancı University

samet.apaydin@sabanciuniv.edu

I am a political scientist studying migration governance and its domestic political consequences, with a particular focus on Türkiye. My research examines how states govern migration, how migration policies shape electoral behavior, and how migration diplomacy influences relations among origin, transit, and destination countries.

I received my Ph.D. in Political Science from Sabancı University in 2025. My dissertation examined the effects of Türkiye’s migration governance on housing markets and electoral behavior. During my doctoral studies, I was also a Visiting Ph.D. Researcher at the University of Glasgow. I have contributed to several Horizon Europe research consortia focusing on migration, including GAPs, MirreM, and MAGYC.

Research interests: migration governance · migration diplomacy · electoral behavior · spatial data analysis
