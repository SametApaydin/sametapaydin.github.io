Act 1 · Where the concentration sits

Türkiye's concentration fingerprint, one cell at a time

Start with the flat map of the problem: every sector crossed with every partner. In the synthetic 2024 panel the import index reads 142 — after two decades in a flat band near 85 — and the single deepest cell is energy, where one supplier carries 56% of the sector.

Try: sort by a sector, click the energy row, then flip the “EU-27 as one supplier” toggle — the aggregate index jumps from 142 to 197, the methodology decision that matters most.

Act 2 · Why the index says what it says

The index, drawn as plumbing

The aggregate index is a sum: each sector's share of trade times that sector's supplier concentration. This sankey draws every term — total on the left, sectors in the middle, partners on the right. Dark, fat bands are where the index lives; thin pale ones are noise.

Try: the dominant-path screener ranks the terms and shows the cumulative percentage — a handful of sector–partner paths carry most of the headline number.

Open full page ↗
That is the anatomy of a single year. But the defining fact of the synthetic panel — like the real chart pack it echoes — is that the composition moved. ↓
Act 3 · How the risk moved, 1995 → 2024

The risk didn't grow. It migrated.

Every partner as a moving point on the share × concentration plane, trailing thirty years of history. Germany drifts out of the danger quadrant as its export monoculture unwinds (it carried ~55% of 1995 export risk); China and Russia march into it on the import side. The export index falls 77 → 65 while the import index climbs.

Try: press play, then use the biggest-movers screener to rank partners by how far they traveled — the danger-quadrant screener lists who sits there today.
