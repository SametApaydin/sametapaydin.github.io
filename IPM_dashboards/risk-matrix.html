Why a matrix? The wheel shows relationships and the map shows geography, but only the matrix shows every cell at once — nothing is hidden behind an aggregate. It is where the analytical work happens: sorting columns turns "China is big" into "China is big in exactly these four sectors"; the EU toggle shows how much the index depends on what counts as one supplier (chart-pack story 13); and the screener extracts the defensible sentence — "partner X supplies Y% of sector Z" — that ends up in the report. Aggregate index shown is Σ(sector share × sector HHI), the chart pack's construction without the elasticity term.
