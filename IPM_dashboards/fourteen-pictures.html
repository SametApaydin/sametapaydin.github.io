Türkiye's import concentration, 1995–2024

index ×1000 · synthetic
Scene 1 / 7

Thirty years, one line

For two decades Türkiye's import concentration drifted inside a narrow band — then climbed steeply after 2019. Anyone reading only this line sees one late surge and twenty quiet years. The line is telling the truth — and hiding the bigger story.

Scene 2 / 7

Underneath, everything changed

Decompose the same index by partner and the flat line explodes. Germany and the EU fall for three decades; China rises from nothing; Russia holds and then surges. The concentration didn't grow — it changed owners. This is the finding the whole project hangs on.

Scene 3 / 7

The export mirror

Run the same index on exports and the story inverts. In 1995 Türkiye was an apparel-to-Germany monoculture; today no single buyer dominates. Export risk simply fell — the one unambiguous success in the panel, and the reason the import side stands out.

Scene 4 / 7

Size is not risk

Compare each partner's share of trade (pale) with their share of risk (dark). Germany is big and exactly proportional. China and Russia carry roughly twice their weight — because what they supply is concentrated. This is what the squared term in the HHI is for.

Scene 5 / 7

What counts as one supplier?

Methodology is a lever. Count the EU-27 as a single supplier — one customs union, one sanctions policy — and Türkiye's measured concentration jumps by half. The index is honest only if this choice is visible, so the real dashboard exposes it as a toggle.

Scene 6 / 7

The chokepoints a value-weighted index can't see

Some dependence is invisible to any aggregate: sectors where one partner supplies more than half of everything. Small money, absolute leverage. These cells are flagged, not weighted — a screener, not a chart.

Scene 7 / 7

What Ankara holds

Dependence tables turn both ways. On the export side sit the sectors where Türkiye is the concentrated buyer's problem — niches where one customer takes most of what Türkiye sells, and mirrors where the leverage is Ankara's. A dependence index worth publishing shows both directions.
