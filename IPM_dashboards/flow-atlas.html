Why a map at all? Because for Türkiye geography is not decoration: the dependence problem has a neighborhood structure (Russian energy from the north, EU machinery from the west, Gulf fuels from the south, Chinese electronics arriving as the one long corridor). A treemap can't show that the risk rotated from west to north-east; this view makes the rotation literal. Click any node or corridor to isolate the dyad and see its 30-year share history. Graticule map, no borders drawn — the corridors, not the countries, are the data.
